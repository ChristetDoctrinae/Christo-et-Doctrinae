Submissions

We welcome theological essays, creative writing, poetry, and visual art from students, scholars, and artists.

Christo et Doctrinae is always looking for serious work. We are not interested in safe pieties or tidy conclusions. We want writing that risks something — that follows an argument or an image wherever it leads, trusting that truth is not fragile.

We accept work in two broad categories: scholarly and critical writing (theology, philosophy, church history, ethics, cultural criticism) and creative work (poetry, personal essays, short fiction, and visual art). Both categories are equally important to us. We believe the distinction between them is often less clear than we pretend.

Submissions are reviewed on a rolling basis. We aim to respond within eight weeks. If your work is accepted for our print edition, you will be contacted separately regarding layout and formatting.
