Write for the Journal

Submissions

We welcome theological essays, creative writing, poetry, and visual art from students, scholars, and artists.

Christo et Doctrinae is always looking for serious work. We are not interested in safe pieties or tidy conclusions. We want writing that risks something — that follows an argument or an image wherever it leads, trusting that truth is not fragile.

We accept work in two broad categories: scholarly and critical writing (theology, philosophy, church history, ethics, cultural criticism) and creative work (poetry, personal essays, short fiction, and visual art). Both categories are equally important to us. We believe the distinction between them is often less clear than we pretend.

Submissions are reviewed on a rolling basis. We aim to respond within eight weeks. If your work is accepted for our print edition, you will be contacted separately regarding layout and formatting.

Current Deadline: Submissions for our Fall 2025 digital edition are open through August 15, 2025. Submissions for the Spring 2026 print edition will open in October 2025.

Before You Submit

Submission Guidelines

Scholarly & Critical Writing

  • Essays should be between 2,000 and 6,000 words. Shorter pieces (500–1,500 words) may be submitted as "Notes & Reflections."
  • Submissions must include a title, an abstract of 150–200 words, and a brief author biography.
  • Please use Chicago footnote style for citations.
  • Avoid excessive technical jargon. We aim for an educated general audience, not solely specialists.
  • Work should engage seriously with primary or secondary sources where relevant.
  • We welcome work from a variety of Christian traditions and perspectives.
  • Previously published work will not be considered unless significantly revised.
  • Files should be submitted as .docx or .pdf. Please remove identifying information from the document itself for blind review.

Creative Writing & Poetry

  • Poetry: submit up to five poems in a single document.
  • Short fiction and personal essays: up to 5,000 words. Flash pieces under 1,000 words are welcome.
  • Creative nonfiction and memoir are particularly welcome — we are drawn to work that thinks as it remembers.
  • We are not looking for explicitly devotional or inspirational verse. We want work that earns its faith through form and language.
  • Work need not be explicitly Christian in theme, but should engage meaningfully with questions of faith, beauty, suffering, or transcendence.
  • Files as .docx or .pdf. Standard manuscript format for prose.

Visual Art & Photography

  • We accept original visual art, photography, and graphic work for both print and digital publication.
  • Digital files should be submitted at minimum 300 DPI (for print) or 1200px wide (for web).
  • File formats: .jpg, .png, or .tiff for photographs and digital art; .pdf for illustrated work.
  • Include a title, medium, dimensions (if applicable), and a brief artist statement (100–200 words).
  • By submitting, you confirm you hold rights to the work and grant us permission to publish.
  • We may contact you regarding cropping or color adjustments for print.

Eligibility & Process

  • We welcome submissions from undergraduate and graduate students, faculty, and the broader community of Christian scholarship.
  • All scholarly submissions undergo blind peer review by at least two editors.
  • Creative submissions are reviewed by our Arts & Letters editorial team.
  • We aim to respond within eight weeks of receipt.
  • Authors retain copyright to their work. We ask for first publication rights.
  • Questions? Email submissions@christoetdoctrinae.org.
