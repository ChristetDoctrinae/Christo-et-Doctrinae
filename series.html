Series

Sustained reflection on questions that do not admit of easy answers — gathered across issues and years.

Articles Series Print Editions

A series in Christo et Doctrinae is not a special issue but a long conversation — one that unfolds across multiple authors, issues, and years, gathering voices that complement and sometimes contest one another.

I
Ongoing

Theology & Culture

14 articles · 2020–present

How does the Gospel transform the culture it enters — and how does culture, in turn, shape the communities that bear it? This series brings theology into conversation with film, music, literature, and contemporary political life, asking what it means to confess Christ in a particular time and place.

Contributors have addressed the theology of the novel, worship in the age of entertainment, the political theology of John Calvin, and the strange kinship between bluegrass music and the Psalms.

II
Ongoing

The Saints

9 articles · 2021–present

Portraits of men and women whose lives have illuminated the Christian way — ancient and modern, celebrated and largely forgotten. Each entry in this series takes a single figure and asks what they saw, what they suffered, and what they have to offer those who come after them.

Subjects have included Julian of Norwich, Simone Weil, Dietrich Bonhoeffer, Thomas à Kempis, and the unknown author of The Cloud of Unknowing.

III
Ongoing

Faith & Reason

11 articles · 2021–present

Is there a unity to what we know by faith and what we know by inquiry? This series explores the relationship between theology and the natural sciences, the philosophy of mind, historical scholarship, and the analytic tradition in philosophy of religion.

The series has ranged from Aquinas and the Five Ways to contemporary arguments about consciousness, fine-tuning, and the problem of religious disagreement.

Read in This Series
IV
Ongoing

Christian Ethics Today

8 articles · 2022–present

The contested moral questions of the present moment examined through the resources of the Christian moral tradition. Contributors take seriously both the complexity of particular ethical questions and the depth of the tradition — and refuse easy answers in either direction.

Articles in this series have addressed poverty and the prophets, the ethics of AI, end-of-life care, racial justice and the Church, and the theology of political engagement.

V
New · 2024

Liturgy & Life

3 articles · 2024–present

A new series exploring the formative power of Christian practice — how the rituals, rhythms, and disciplines of Christian life shape those who inhabit them. This series is interested in the body as much as the mind, in practice as much as proposition.

Opening articles have addressed fasting, the daily office, and the architecture of worship spaces as a form of embodied theology.

Read in This Series
VI
New · 2025

Reading Scripture

2 articles · 2025–present

An invitation to dwell with the biblical text in ways that resist both historicist reduction and easy allegorizing. Each piece in this series takes a biblical passage — a narrative, a psalm, an epistle — and reads it with sustained attention, theological depth, and literary care.

We are looking for writers who can hold together the world of the text and the world of the reader without collapsing either. Proposals welcome.

For Prospective Contributors

Propose a Series

We occasionally launch new series when a question seems to warrant sustained, multi-author attention. If you have an idea for a series — a theme, a question, a set of texts — we want to hear it. Proposals should include a brief description of the series, the kinds of articles it might include, and why you think it belongs in these pages.
