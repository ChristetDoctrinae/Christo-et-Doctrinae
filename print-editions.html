Spring 2025

Volume III

Love & Justice

The third volume of Christo et Doctrinae gathers around a conviction: that love and justice, long set in opposition, are in the Christian moral vision inseparable. To love is to will justice; to pursue justice without love is to pursue an abstraction. This volume does not resolve the tension so much as inhabit it — with rigor, with care, and with a kind of stubborn hope.

The volume includes essays on agape and its secular imitators, on the prophetic tradition and contemporary poverty, on the theology of criminal justice reform, and on love as a mode of knowing. It also includes poetry, a short story, and original artwork by student contributors.

Contents: 9 essays · 5 poems · 1 short story · 12 pages of artwork · 88 pages total · Perfect bound · Designed by Sophia Reinholt
