Why It Matters

What Your Gift Supports

Christo et Doctrinae is entirely student-run and relies on the generosity of individuals who believe that serious Christian thought has a place in university life. We receive no direct university funding. Every dollar we raise goes directly to producing the journal.

The Print Edition

Our annual print edition is the heart of what we do. It is designed, typeset, and produced by student editors, and printed in a limited run that is distributed on campus and by subscription. A single print run costs approximately $2,400. Gifts at any level help make this possible.

Student Stipends

We believe student editors should be compensated for their work. Our editorial board contributes hundreds of hours each year to the journal. Donor gifts allow us to offer modest stipends to our senior editors, making the work accessible to students who might otherwise not be able to take it on.

Guest Lectures & Events

Each year we host a lecture by a theologian, artist, or writer whose work exemplifies what we care about. Past guests have included novelists, philosophers, church historians, and practicing artists. These events are open to the whole university and free to attend.

Open Access

Every article we publish is freely available on our website. We are committed to making Christian thought accessible to anyone who wants it — not only to those who can afford a subscription. Donor support makes this possible.
