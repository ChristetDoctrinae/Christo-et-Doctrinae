Who We Are

About the Journal

A community of students, scholars, and artists in pursuit of Christian understanding.

Editorial Statement

Our Mission

Christo et Doctrinae — "for Christ and for learning" — takes its name from the motto of Furman University. It is the conviction of this journal that the two cannot finally be separated. To learn well is to orient one's mind toward truth; to follow Christ is to follow the one who said, I am the truth.

The journal was founded in 2020 by a small group of students who believed that the intellectual and spiritual life of the university needed a venue — not for devotional newsletters or chapel bulletins, but for serious, sustained engagement with the questions that faith raises and the questions that reason cannot silence.

We are interested in theology as a discipline, but we are equally interested in the theological imagination as it works in fiction, poetry, visual art, music, and memoir. We believe that Flannery O'Connor's stories are doing theology. We believe that Bach's cantatas are doing theology. We believe that the student who sits at her desk at two in the morning wrestling with the problem of evil is doing theology.

We publish twice yearly — a digital edition in the fall and a print edition in the spring. We welcome submissions from Furman undergraduates and graduate students, Furman faculty, and occasionally from the broader community of Christian scholarship.

What We Publish

We publish essays, articles, and book reviews in theology, philosophy of religion, church history, and Christian ethics. We also publish creative work — poetry, short fiction, personal essays, and visual art — that engages seriously with the Christian tradition or with the questions faith raises.

We are not a confessional journal in a narrow sense. We do not require contributors to affirm a particular creed, and we welcome perspectives from across the Christian tradition — Catholic, Protestant, Orthodox, and everything in between. What we ask is that contributors engage the tradition with genuine intellectual seriousness and with the kind of care that the subject demands.

We do not want to be a journal that Christians happen to read. We want to be a journal that Christians — and anyone curious about what Christians think — cannot afford to ignore.

Our History

The journal was established in spring 2020 with a single photocopied issue distributed in the university's theology department. That first issue contained three essays and a poem. It was read, apparently, by about forty people.

By fall 2021, we had moved to a designed print edition and a website. By 2023, we were receiving submissions from across the country. The print edition — produced in a limited run and distributed on campus and by subscription — has become a point of pride for the journal and for the community it serves.

We remain, at heart, what we always were: a small community of people who take seriously the claim that faith and learning belong together.
